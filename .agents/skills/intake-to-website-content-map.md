# Agent Skill: Intake to Website Content Map

Use this doc to analyze a completed business intake form and convert it into a website-ready content strategy, copy map, and wireframe planning summary.

This is not a final copywriting task. The goal is to translate intake answers into practical website structure, content hierarchy, messaging priorities, lead-capture recommendations, trust messaging, FAQ direction, visual direction, and copy guardrails.

## Core Rules

Treat the intake form as the source of truth.

Do not invent unsupported claims, certifications, guarantees, review counts, pricing, awards, financing, emergency availability, years in business, warranties, service promises, or operational details unless they are clearly present in the intake.

Reasonable assumptions are allowed only when needed to make the plan usable. Label them clearly as assumptions.

Do not merely summarize the intake. Convert each section into practical website planning decisions.

## Primary Objective

Turn raw intake answers into a website planning summary that can guide:

* Homepage structure
* Service page structure
* Copywriting direction
* Wireframe section order
* CTA strategy
* Contact form fields
* FAQ content
* Trust messaging
* Local SEO basics
* Follow-up automation logic
* Visual/image direction
* Copy boundaries

## Intake Translation Framework

Use this table as the analysis framework for turning intake answers into website content, copy strategy, and wireframe guidance.

| Intake section     | What it gives you                  | Website content it creates                                               |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Business Basics    | Name, contact, hours, address, GBP | Header, footer, contact page, local SEO basics, schema-ish business info |
| Main Services      | What they actually sell            | Service cards, services page, form dropdowns, homepage hierarchy         |
| Priority Services  | What they want more of             | Hero focus, featured services, landing page ideas, CTA direction         |
| Service Area       | Where they want leads              | Service area section, local copy, location pages later                   |
| Lead Flow          | How leads should behave            | CTA labels, form type, thank-you copy, automation logic                  |
| Trust Points       | Why people should believe them     | Trust bar, why choose us, about page, proof bullets                      |
| Customer Questions | Objections/friction                | FAQ, service page sections, form helper text                             |
| Assets / Offers    | Visual and promo inputs            | Image direction, seasonal banner, landing page ideas                     |
| Final Details      | Boundaries                         | What not to claim, what not to promote, tone guardrails                  |

Use the intake sections this way:

* Business Basics → header, footer, contact, hours, NAP, and local business info.
* Main Services → service cards, services page structure, homepage hierarchy, and form dropdowns.
* Priority Services → hero focus, featured sections, landing pages, and CTA emphasis.
* Service Area → service area copy, local SEO language, and future location page ideas.
* Lead Flow → CTA labels, form behavior, thank-you copy, and automation logic.
* Trust Points → trust bars, why choose us sections, about page themes, and reassurance copy.
* Customer Questions → FAQs, objection-handling sections, helper text, and friction-reducing content.
* Assets / Offers → image direction, promo banners, seasonal offers, and landing page ideas.
* Final Details → claims to avoid, services not to promote, tone limits, and other copy guardrails.

## Analysis Method

Read the intake once for facts. Read it again for strategic implications.

For each intake section, determine:

1. What the client explicitly said.
2. What it implies for the website.
3. Where the information should appear.
4. How important it is to conversion.
5. Whether it affects copy, wireframe, CTA, form, FAQ, trust, SEO, visuals, or automation.
6. Which claims or services should be avoided.
7. What information is missing and should be flagged.

## Prioritization Rule

Do not treat every intake answer as equally important.

Identify which services, trust points, customer questions, lead actions, and boundaries matter most for conversion. Use those to shape hierarchy, section order, CTA placement, and homepage emphasis.

When completeness conflicts with clarity, prioritize the content most likely to help a visitor understand the business, trust it, and take action.

## Required Output Format

Use the structure below.

### Website Content Summary

Briefly describe what kind of website the business needs, what the site should emphasize, and what customer action it should drive.

### Key Website Priorities

List the most important strategic priorities based on the intake.

Examples:

* Make urgent leads easy to call.
* Feature priority services more heavily than lower-priority services.
* Emphasize local trust and professional service.
* Capture quote requests through a simple mobile-first form.
* Use service area language throughout the homepage.

### Content / Copy Map

Break down recommended content by page or section.

Include:

* Homepage
* Services overview
* Priority service sections
* About / trust section
* Service area section
* FAQ section
* Contact / quote form
* Thank-you page
* Footer

For each section, include:

* Purpose
* Content to include
* Copy angle
* Intake details that support it

### Homepage Wireframe Guidance

Recommend the homepage section order.

Explain what should appear high on the page, what can appear later, and where CTAs should repeat.

Default structure:

1. Hero
2. Trust bar
3. Priority services
4. Main services overview
5. Why choose us
6. Process / how it works
7. Service area
8. FAQ
9. Final CTA
10. Footer

Modify this order when the intake suggests a better structure.

### Lead Capture Recommendations

Recommend:

* Primary CTA label
* Secondary CTA label
* Urgent service CTA behavior
* Contact form type
* Suggested form fields
* Thank-you page message
* Owner/customer notification logic
* Useful follow-up or reminder logic

Do not overcomplicate the lead flow unless the intake justifies it.

### Trust Messaging

Convert trust points into usable website content.

Organize into:

* Trust bar items
* Why choose us bullets
* About section themes
* Reassurance copy
* Visual proof to show if assets exist

Avoid vague claims like “best service” unless supported. Prefer specific, grounded language.

### FAQ Direction

Use customer questions and objections to recommend FAQ topics.

FAQs should reduce hesitation around:

* Pricing
* Timing
* Service process
* Repair vs replacement
* Emergency availability
* Service area
* Maintenance
* Estimates or quotes

Do not write final FAQ answers unless asked. Summarize each topic and the purpose of the answer.

### Visual / Asset Direction

Recommend image and visual content needs based on the intake.

Include:

* Types of photos needed
* Where photos should appear
* Whether real team/project photos matter
* Promo/offer placement
* Seasonal or landing page opportunities

### Copy Guardrails

List what the website should avoid saying, claiming, or promoting.

Include:

* Unsupported claims
* Services not to promote
* Customer types to avoid attracting
* Pricing or guarantee claims to avoid
* Tone issues
* Operational promises not confirmed by the intake

### Content Gaps / Assumptions

List missing information needed before final copywriting.

Separate:

* Confirmed facts
* Reasonable assumptions
* Open questions

Do not let missing details stop the analysis. Make the best practical recommendation with the available information.

## Tone and Output Quality

Keep the output practical, strategic, website-focused, skimmable, and actionable.

Write for a designer, strategist, or website builder who needs to turn the intake into a content plan and wireframe.

Prefer clear recommendations over exhaustive commentary.

Be specific, grounded, and useful. Do not sound like a generic marketing consultant.

## Do Not

* Do not write final polished website copy unless explicitly asked.
* Do not invent business facts.
* Do not over-focus on SEO at the expense of conversion.
* Do not ignore the client’s boundaries.
* Do not bury priority services.
* Do not make every service seem equally important if the intake says otherwise.
* Do not turn the output into a generic summary of the intake.
